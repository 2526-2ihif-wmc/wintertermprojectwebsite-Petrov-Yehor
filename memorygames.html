Memorable NBA games

VS

THE FLU GAME (1997)

June 11, 1997

NBA Finals Game 5

Chicago Bulls vs Utah Jazz

90-88

Michael Jordan woke up violently ill, barely able to stand. Doctors advised him not to play. He played anyway — scoring 38 points including the go-ahead three-pointer with 25 seconds left. After the final buzzer, Jordan collapsed into Scottie Pippen's arms. This game defined what "Mamba Mentality" means before the term even existed.

VS

THE LAST SHOT (1998)

June 14, 1998

NBA Finals Game 6

Chicago Bulls vs Utah Jazz

87-86

With 5.2 seconds left and the Bulls trailing by one, Jordan stole the ball from Karl Malone, dribbled up court, and hit the most iconic shot in NBA history over Bryon Russell. His sixth championship. His sixth Finals MVP. The perfect ending to a perfect dynasty.

VS

KOBE 81 (2006)

January 22, 2006

Regular Season

Los Angeles Lakers vs Toronto Raptors

122-104

The second-greatest scoring performance in NBA history. Kobe Bryant erupted for 81 points — 55 in the second half alone. He made 28 of 46 shots from the field and single-handedly brought the Lakers back from an 18-point deficit. Only Wilt Chamberlain's 100-point game stands above this masterpiece.

VS

THE BLOCK (2016)

June 19, 2016

NBA Finals Game 7

Cleveland Cavaliers vs Golden State Warriors

93-89

Down 3-1 to the 73-win Warriors, Cleveland did the impossible. With the game tied and under two minutes left, LeBron James chased down Andre Iguodala and pinned his layup against the backboard. Kyrie Irving hit the go-ahead three. Cleveland won its first championship in 52 years. LeBron wept on the court.

MAGIC'S ROOKIE MIRACLE (1980)

May 16, 1980

NBA Finals Game 6

Los Angeles Lakers vs Philadelphia 76ers

123-107

With Kareem Abdul-Jabbar injured, 20-year-old rookie Magic Johnson stepped up. He played all five positions — including center — and delivered one of the greatest Finals performances ever. 42 points, 15 rebounds, 7 assists. A rookie winning Finals MVP. Basketball had never seen anything like it.

VS

RAY ALLEN'S MIRACLE (2013)

June 18, 2013

NBA Finals Game 6

Miami Heat vs San Antonio Spurs

103-100 (OT)

The Spurs were 5 seconds away from the championship. The arena staff had already set up the ropes for the trophy presentation. Then Chris Bosh grabbed an offensive rebound and kicked it to Ray Allen in the corner. His three-pointer tied the game with 5.2 seconds left. Miami won in overtime and took Game 7 to claim the title.
